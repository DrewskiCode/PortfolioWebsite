Facial Recognition · CNN + YOLO

Eye Tracking System

Built a facial landmark detection pipeline that highlights faces and the dominant eye, enabling camera autofocus and real-time tracking use cases.

Overview

The model detects faces and dominant eyes using WFLW landmarks, then generates bounding boxes with confidence scores for tracking and tagging.

Use Cases

Mirrorless Camera Autofocus
Human Detection Systems
Facial Tracking
Photo & Video Tagging

Dataset

WFLW (Wider Facial Landmarks in-the-Wild)

  • 10,000 faces
  • 98 annotated landmarks
  • Attribute annotations for variation

Data Prep

  • Used edge points to bound faces.
  • Used left/right eye landmarks to bound eyes.
  • Created two classes: face + dominant_eye.
  • Split 80/20 training vs validation.

Models

  • YOLO v8 (pretrained, tuned on WFLW).
  • CNN with MobileNetV2 backbone for feature extraction.
  • Bounding box regression with IoU metrics.

Training Highlights

  • YOLO Nano: 640px images, 50 epochs, Adam/AdamW.
  • YOLO Medium: 640px images, 23 epochs, SGD.
  • CNN: 224px images, 100 epochs max, Adam optimizer.

Results

YOLO models achieved strong face/eye detection, while the CNN underperformed due to lower-resolution training data.

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Improve CNN with data augmentation and a custom backbone.
  • Reduce YOLO false negatives on dominant eye detection.
  • Continue training to reduce underfitting.
